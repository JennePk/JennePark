Where to go? 7-night Japan itinerary

By Jenne PK · April 2026 · 10 min read

When I started planning my trip to Japan, I had no idea where to go. Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka… everything looked amazing, but overwhelming.

So I built a simple route — realistic, beginner-friendly, and balanced. This is the itinerary I’m personally using for my solo trip.

7-night recommended route

A balanced journey through modern cities, tradition, nature, and food.

  • Nights 1–2 — Tokyo → Shinjuku, Harajuku, Akihabara, TeamLab
  • Night 3 — Hakone → Mt Fuji views, onsen, ryokan stay
  • Nights 4–5 — Kyoto → Fushimi Inari, Gion, bamboo forest
  • Night 6 — Nara → Deer park, Todai-ji temple
  • Night 7 — Osaka → Dotonbori, street food night

Why this route works

This itinerary works because it doesn’t try to do everything. It focuses on experience, not exhaustion.

You move from modern chaos → nature → tradition → culture → food.

Tokyo — first impression of Japan

Tokyo feels like another world. Neon lights, silent trains, giant crossings, and tiny peaceful streets just next to chaos.

If it’s your first time, this is where Japan really “hits you”.

Hakone — pause & nature

After Tokyo, Hakone feels like breathing again. Hot springs, mountains, and if you're lucky — Mt Fuji.

This is where you slow down and reset.

Kyoto — the emotional highlight

Kyoto feels like stepping into a movie. Temples, wooden streets, bamboo forests, quiet mornings.

If Japan had a soul, Kyoto might be it.

Nara — simplicity

Nara is calm, green, and surprisingly emotional. Deer walking freely, ancient temples, soft silence.

Osaka — food & energy

Osaka is the opposite of Kyoto: loud, fun, alive. Street food, neon lights, and a very local vibe.

Final thoughts

This itinerary is not about seeing everything. It’s about feeling Japan in layers.

If I had to describe it simply:

“7 nights, 5 worlds, one unforgettable journey.”
